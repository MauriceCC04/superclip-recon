SuperCLIP-Recon

Does masked caption-token reconstruction help a COCO-scale SuperCLIP-style baseline? A small, retrieval-grounded test of a lightweight auxiliary loss.

MS-COCO Captions · ViT-B/32 (CLIP)
5 matched seeds · 10 epochs each
The gap

Token classification is bag-of-words-like.

  • CLIP aligns image and text globally; fine-grained caption content is only indirectly supervised.
  • SuperCLIP adds token-presence supervision — strong gains, but still set-of-words.
  • "a dog chasing a red ball" and "a red ball near a dog" share tokens but describe different scenes.
  • This matches known compositional weaknesses on ARO and Winoground.
Contribution

A small, retrieval-grounded test.

  • Baseline. COCO-scale SuperCLIP-style anchor: CLIP contrastive + token-classification (top-1000 COCO vocab, frequency-reweighted).
  • Extension (ReconA). A 2-layer MLP with 12 learned slot embeddings predicts masked caption tokens from the image embedding only. No decoder, no text context.
  • Evidence. Matched-seed COCO retrieval (5 seeds) + ARO + Winoground + small λ / mask-rate diagnostics.

Why this is lightweight
  • One MLP head, no decoder, no extra backbone, no extra dataset.
  • Image features alone predict the masked tokens — no text context.
  • Observed runtime overhead ≈ 0.9% (3648 → 3680 s).

Variant B (phrase reconstruction)

Exploratory: 1 seed, retrieval only. λ=0.1 and λ=0.5 both within ±0.2 R-sum of baseline. ARO and Winoground were not evaluated for Variant B; structured phrase masking is left for future work with paired seeds and full compositional probes.

ReconA's runtime profile makes the loss easy to integrate at this scale. Whether the small mean retrieval gain holds at larger scale, with full-vocab targets, or with structured phrase masking, remains the open question.

What we found · honest reading

  • ReconA is retrieval-compatible and lightweight (≈ 0.9% overhead).
  • Mean ΔR-sum = +0.317 — but 95% CI crosses zero; only 3/5 seeds positive.
  • Compositional probes shift slightly, with direction inconsistent (ARO Rel regresses).

What it is · not

  • Not a full caption-generation model — no decoder, no autoregression.
  • Not a guaranteed retrieval improvement — careful negative-to-mixed result.
  • Not a SuperCLIP reproduction at original scale (top-1000 vocab, COCO only).

What's next · if we had more compute

  • Larger-scale runs with full CLIP-vocab token classification targets.
  • Structured phrase masking (Variant B done properly, with paired seeds).
  • Stronger compositional benchmarks (e.g. SugarCrepe) and more paired seeds.
